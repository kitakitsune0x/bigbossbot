## Design Context

### Users
OSINT analysts working at desks during extended monitoring sessions. They need confidence that the data is live and accurate, a sense of control over what they're watching, and calm focus that doesn't fatigue over hours. The interface is a professional tool, not a spectacle.

### Brand Personality
Sharp, dark, tactical. Military command post energy — dark surfaces, crisp typography, accent colors that carry meaning. Controlled intensity without theatrics.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark, high-contrast. Deep dark backgrounds with sharp type and strategic use of color for severity and status. Reference: Bloomberg Terminal, Grafana dark mode. The density of a Reuters terminal with the clarity of a well-designed ops dashboard.

Anti-references:
- Generic SaaS dashboards (no cookie-cutter card grids, no bland everything-looks-the-same energy)
- Sci-fi movie props (no scanlines, no glowing cyan, no fake radar animations — the old theme)
- Cluttered news aggregators (scannable, not overwhelming)
- Over-minimalist (don't sacrifice information density for whitespace — this is an ops tool)

### Design Principles
1. **Data earns the space** — every element exists because an analyst needs it. No decorative filler.
2. **Color means something** — red is a threat, amber is caution, green is clear. Never use color for decoration.
3. **Density without clutter** — tight spacing, clear hierarchy, scannable at a glance. The Bloomberg test: can you find what you need in under 2 seconds?
4. **Quiet confidence** — the interface should feel like it was designed by someone who actually uses intel tools, not by someone who watched a movie about them.
